ABOUT KERALA TOURISM

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a picturesque state located on the southwestern coast of India. It is known for its lush greenery, serene backwaters, pristine beaches, and vibrant culture.

Kerala boasts a rich cultural heritage, with influences from various civilizations and religions. The state is renowned for its classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, traditional music, colorful festivals, and delectable cuisine.

The backwaters of Kerala, consisting of a network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and lagoons, offer visitors a tranquil and scenic experience. Houseboat cruises through the backwaters are a popular tourist attraction, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in Kerala's natural beauty while enjoying traditional hospitality and cuisine.

In addition to its natural beauty and cultural heritage, Kerala is known for its emphasis on education and healthcare. The state boasts high literacy rates and quality healthcare facilities, making it one of the most progressive states in India.
